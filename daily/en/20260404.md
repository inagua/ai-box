## 📈 AI Trend
**On-Device Small Language Models (SLMs)**
As hardware becomes more specialized, there is a major shift towards running smaller, optimized models directly on consumer devices. This approach offers several advantages: privacy is significantly improved as data never leaves the device, latency is nearly non-existent, and it works without an internet connection.

Technologically, this is made possible by techniques like advanced quantization (4-bit or even 1.5-bit) and distillation, where larger "teacher" models train smaller "student" models. We're seeing companies like Microsoft, Google, and Apple all releasing SLMs that punch way above their weight class in terms of parameter count versus performance.

The evolution of this trend points towards a future where every personal device has a unique, locally-fine-tuned assistant that understands the user's personal context without compromising their data security. This is a departure from the "centralized cloud" model that has dominated the first few years of the AI boom.
- *Status: Volatile*

## 💡 Key Concept: Liquid Neural Networks
Liquid Neural Networks (LNNs) represent a fundamental shift from static neural architectures to models with dynamic, time-continuous equations. These models are inspired by the biological brains of small organisms like C. elegans and can adapt their behavior in real-time.
