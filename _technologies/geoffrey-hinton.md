Geoffrey Hinton is a world-renowned computer scientist and psychologist, widely considered one of the "godfathers of deep learning." His pioneering research on artificial neural networks has been instrumental in the development of modern AI technologies.

## Key Contributions

- **Backpropagation**: He was one of the first researchers to demonstrate the effectiveness of the backpropagation algorithm for training multi-layer neural networks.
- **Boltzmann Machines**: He co-invented Boltzmann machines, a type of stochastic recurrent neural network.
- **AlexNet**: He was a co-author of the seminal paper on AlexNet, which revolutionized the field of computer vision and triggered the current deep learning boom.
- **Capsule Networks**: He proposed capsule networks as a way to better model hierarchical relationships in visual data.

## Recognition and Advocacy

Hinton is a recipient of the **Turing Award** (often referred to as the "Nobel Prize of Computing") and the **Nobel Prize in Physics**. After decades at Google and the University of Toronto, he has recently focused on raising awareness about the potential existential risks associated with the rapid advancement of artificial general intelligence (AGI).
